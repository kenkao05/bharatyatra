Guwahati

Gateway to Assam with ancient temples and river islands

Temples & Religious Sites

Kamakhya Temple

About: One of the most famous Shakti Peeths of India, dedicated to Goddess Kamakhya. Located on Nilachal Hill, overlooking the Brahmaputra River. Known for the Ambubachi Mela.

Things to Do: Temple darshan, panoramic view of Guwahati and river, nearby small shrines.

Nearby Hotels:

Wide range in Guwahati – Vivanta Guwahati, Hotel Rajmahal, Mayflower Hotel, budget lodges near Paltan Bazar

Nearby Restaurants:

Restaurants available at GS Road and Fancy Bazar

View on Google Maps

Umananda Temple

About: A small Shiva temple on Peacock (Umananda) Island, right in the middle of the Brahmaputra River.

Things to Do: Short ferry/boat ride, temple darshan, enjoy river views.

Nearby Hotels:

Same options as Guwahati city (Paltan Bazar, Panbazar)

Nearby Restaurants:

Same options as Guwahati city (Paltan Bazar, Panbazar)

View on Google Maps
Historical Sites

Hayagriva Madhava Temple

About: Ancient Vaishnavite temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu (as Hayagriva). Important pilgrimage site for Hindus and Buddhists.

Things to Do: Visit the temple, explore Hajo town (also has Muslim shrines & Buddhist sites).

Nearby Hotels:

Small hotels/guesthouses in Hajo. Many visitors stay in Guwahati (approx. 30 km away)

Nearby Restaurants:

Local eateries in Hajo town

Kaziranga National Park

UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for one-horned rhinoceros

National Parks & Wildlife

Kaziranga National Park

About: UNESCO World Heritage Site, world-famous for the one-horned Indian rhinoceros. Also home to tigers, elephants, swamp deer, and migratory birds.

Things to Do: Jeep safari, elephant safari, bird-watching, cultural village visits.

Nearby Hotels:

Budget: Bonhabi Resort, Kaziranga Guesthouse
Mid-range: Landmark Woods, Infinity Kaziranga
Luxury: IORA Retreat, Wild Grass Lodge

Nearby Restaurants:

Hotel restaurants and local eateries

Manas National Park

Tiger Reserve and UNESCO Site with diverse wildlife

National Parks & Wildlife

Manas National Park

About: UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for elephants, tigers, leopards, rhinos, and rare birds. Also a Project Tiger Reserve.

Things to Do: Jeep safari, rafting on Manas River, bird-watching, cultural tours.

Nearby Hotels:

Florican Cottages, Musa Jungle Retreat, Manas Jungle Camp

Nearby Restaurants:

Jungle resort restaurants and local food

View on Google Maps

Majuli Island

World's largest river island with rich cultural heritage

Cultural & Heritage Sites

Majuli Island

About: World's largest river island on the Brahmaputra. Famous for Neo-Vaishnavite Satras (monasteries), traditional art, handloom, and culture.

Things to Do: Visit Satras, watch Sattriya dance, mask-making workshops, bird-watching, experience village life.

Nearby Hotels:

Mostly homestays & eco-lodges – La Maison de Ananda, Dekasang Resort. Best to book in advance.

Nearby Restaurants:

Local Assamese cuisine in homestays and lodges

Travel & Stay Tips

Best Time to Visit

November – April (pleasant weather + national parks open). Avoid monsoon (floods & park closures).

Booking Tips

For Kaziranga, Manas & Majuli, book safaris/homestays in advance.

Local Food

Assamese thali, fish curry (Masor Tenga), Pitha sweets. Guwahati, Tezpur & Sivasagar have good restaurants. In Majuli & parks, expect simple local food.

Transport

Guwahati is the main entry (airport, rail hub). Kaziranga (5–6 hrs drive from Guwahati). Sivasagar (train/road from Dibrugarh/Jorhat). Majuli (ferry from Jorhat).
